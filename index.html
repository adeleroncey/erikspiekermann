Erik Spiekermann was born in 1947 in North Germany. Erik Spiekermann is a typographer, type designer, and author. He is the type designer who, and with other collaborations, designed typefaces FF Meta, ITC Officina, FF Info, FF Unit, LoType, and Berliner Grotesk in addition to many exclusive corporate typefaces including custom type for The Economist, Deutsche Bahn, Cisco, Bosch, Mozilla, Autodesk. He is a board member of two organisations: ISTD (International Society of Typographic Designers) and IIID (International Institute for Information Design). He was awarded high end honoraries in graphic design and typography nationwide.

He studied Art History and English at the Free University of Berlin. When he was 12 years old he started working at a print shop. Over the years he was an apprentice in Berlin and learned how to typeset and print properly while going to highschool. In 1977, when his print shop burnt down, he continued to sketch for other people for them to work from but also photosetting and marking up type for typesetters. Moving to London, he worked for agencies in corporate design and signage projects as a graphic consultant coming up with typographic solutions for companies. He worked for Film Composition, Wolff Olins, and other consultancies. In 1979, he returned to Berlin to co-found his agency, MetaDesign. Originally, Meta Design was created because he and his group were asked to design for design. Creating guidelines for other designers to work form. Today, Germany’s largest design firm, with offices in Berlin, London, and San Francisco with corporate design projects and clients such as Audi, Skoda, Berlin Transit, Düsseldorf Airport… In 1988, he started FontShop, a production and distribution of electronic fonts company and another influential FontFont typeface library with Joan Spiekermann. In 2001, he left Meta Design to now run a company called edenspiekermann with offices in Berlin, Amsterdam, London, Stuttgart, San Francisco and Singapore with Susanna Dulkinys. Edenspiekermann is an agency for strategy, design and communication. The agency is the result of a merger in 2009 between Eden design & communication Amsterdam and SpiekermannPartners Berlin. Since 2013, he has run and founded p98a. in Berlin, a typographic workshop dedicated to post-digital letterpress printing.

FF Real

Erik Spikermann designed this font in collaboration with Ralph Oliver du Carrois. In 2017, both designers created styles for families FF Real Head and FF Real Text expanding to a total of 52 styles with newly designed italics. FF Real Text is a typeface designed with a large x-height with open counters and a slightly structured character. It is available in 13 finely graded weights, from Hairline to Black. Erik Spiekermann not only produced his font digitally, but also had it cut into wood at a size of 16 cicero.

FF Meta

Initially released as a commercial font in 1991 by Spiekermann's company FontFont, FF Meta is a sans serif typeface family composed of over sixty fonts. The design has the requirement to not be confused with Helvetica. Part of the original demand was to produce a typeface that could be printed almost anywhere without extra typesetting and low printing costs. In 1992, they released a second version of FF Meta called FF Meta 2 Family and until 1998 released variations into FF Meta Family such as FF Meta Normal to FF Meta Black which are each available in roman, italic, small caps and italic small caps.

FF Unit

Originally conceived as a variant of FF Meta and as the house typeface for Spiekermann’s “United Designer” studio, FF Unit became a distinct family. FF Unit is available in seven weights, from Thin to Ultra, each accompanied by the corresponding italic. In addition to the sans serifs, Spikermann creates two extra variant styles FF Unit Slab, which include seven different weights and their italic versions , and FF Unit Rounded, with rounded terminals individually adapted for overall warm and playful characters. FF Unit have compact and more squat characters with forms based on superellipse similar to a modern square sans. He abandoned the oblique finials he had in FF Meta. FF Unit is available in seven weights, from Thin to Ultra, each accompanied by the corresponding italic.

ITC Officina

The ITC Officina Super Family was designed by Erik Spiekermann and Ole Schäfer and published by ITC. Erik Spiekermann first conceived of the ITC Officina typeface design in the early 1980s. Its original intention was to create a family of type that is suitable for office correspondence and business documentation however its use was far beyond that intention. IT Officina offers five different weights in both the Serif and Sans Serif versions with corresponding italics. Its intention was to create a family of type that is suitable for office correspondence and business documentation. ITC Officina is legible at small sizes perfect for documents because of its strong serifs and full counters. It also has an economical use of space with its condensed font design.
